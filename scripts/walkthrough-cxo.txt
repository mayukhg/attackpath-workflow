CONTEXTUAL RISK SCORING — SMART, NOT JUST LOUD
Reference: Contextual Risk Scoring
----------------------------------------

  "Standard vulnerability scanners give every critical CVE the same score. We
  don't. Our contextual risk engine adjusts scores based on where an asset sits in
  the attack chain."

  "This API Gateway? Its base CVSS score is 73. But because it's a chokepoint —
  internet-facing, part of a toxic combination, on three attack paths — we elevate
  it to 97. That's a 24% increase. It deserves more attention, and now it gets it."

  "Conversely, a Dev Test Server with a base score of 62 gets suppressed to 27
  because it's isolated — no lateral movement possible, no path to a crown jewel.
  Your team won't waste time on it."

  KEY MESSAGE: We score based on actual business risk, not just CVSS. Your team
  focuses on what actually matters.

================================================================================
APPENDIX — EXECUTIVE Q&A TALKING POINTS
================================================================================

Q: "How is this different from our existing vulnerability scanner?"
A: Scanners find individual vulnerabilities. We show how they chain together into
   exploitable attack paths. A CVE with no path to a crown jewel is very different
   from one that sits on three attack paths leading to your Finance DB.

Q: "What does 'crown jewel' mean in this context?"
A: Any asset your organization defines as business-critical — production databases
   with PII, financial systems, customer data. You define them; we map every attack
   path that leads to them.

Q: "How does the ROI number get calculated?"
A: Risk Reduction Value divided by Remediation Cost. The platform factors in
   severity, blast radius, exploitability, and effort to produce a normalized
   multiplier.

Q: "Can this integrate with our ticketing system?"
A: Yes — Create Ticket actions can integrate with your ITSM (ServiceNow, Jira,
   etc.) directly from the Remediate tab.

Q: "What happens if we can't fix something immediately?"
A: Suppress with a documented justification. The accepted risk is recorded with a
   timestamp and owner — maintaining your audit trail and compliance posture.

Q: "How often does the data refresh?"
A: The attack path graph refreshes as your asset and vulnerability data updates in
   the Qualys platform — continuously, not periodically.
